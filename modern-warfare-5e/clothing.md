# Clothing

Everyday and operational apparel for Operators. Clothing is always worn under [armor](armor.md) - an Operator never has
a bare chest or feet beneath a plate carrier. Clothing doesn't contribute AC on its own; instead, every item carries
tags that the GM uses to rule on situational advantage/disadvantage.

Protective footwear and gloves live here too, with a separate stat schema. See [Armor](armor.md) for torso armor,
helmets, and plates.

## Quick Outfits

Players who don't want to pick individual items can grab a pre-built outfit. Each covers head-to-toe (minus footwear and
gloves) and reflects a common archetype - answering *why* the character is dressed this way, not just *what the weather
is*. Layer extra pieces from the sections below for unusual conditions.

## Tags

| Axis    | Tag             | Effect                                                                                                      |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Climate | warm            | Ignores one level of exhaustion from light cold (autumn, mild winter).                                      |
| Climate | insulated       | Ignores one level of exhaustion from extreme cold (arctic, high altitude).                                  |
| Climate | ventilated      | Ignores one level of exhaustion from heat (desert, tropics).                                                |
| Climate | waterproof      | Ignores exposure from rain, spray, and brief submersion.                                                    |
| Camo    | camo-woodland   | Advantage on Stealth in forest/temperate terrain.                                                           |
| Camo    | camo-arid       | Advantage on Stealth in desert/dry terrain.                                                                 |
| Camo    | camo-urban      | Advantage on Stealth in urban/industrial terrain.                                                           |
| Camo    | camo-multicam   | Advantage on Stealth in mixed/transitional terrain.                                                         |
| Camo    | camo-snow       | Advantage on Stealth in snow/arctic terrain.                                                                |
| Social  | civilian        | Advantage on blend-in checks in civilian areas; disadvantage at formal events or uniform-expected settings. |
| Social  | military        | Advantage in military/uniformed settings; disadvantage on civilian blend-in.                                |
| Social  | tactical        | Reads as contractor/operator - mixed social results, neutral-to-hostile in civilian settings.               |
| Social  | formal          | Advantage in diplomatic/high-society settings.                                                              |
| Utility | conceals-pistol | Conceals a holdout or service pistol from casual inspection.                                                |
| Utility | conceals-face   | Hides identity; advantage against identification by witnesses or cameras.                                   |
| Utility | under-armor     | Cut to be worn comfortably under a plate carrier or flak jacket.                                            |

Multiple items with the same climate tag do not stack beyond ignoring one level of exhaustion. A base layer plus a parka
together counts as `insulated`.

Ballistic eyewear grants resistance to flashbang and debris-based blindness at GM discretion.
