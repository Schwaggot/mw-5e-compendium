# Mental Fitness Programs

Mental fitness programs cover book-learning, interrogation resistance, social polish, and language work. They raise the
three mental abilities, shore up mental saves, and broaden an operator's skill list.

## Education Training 1-4

Academic study and technical schooling. Each level increases your Intelligence score by 1 (respecting your racial or
class maximum).

## Resolve Training 1-4

Mental discipline and stress inoculation. Each level increases your Wisdom score by 1 (respecting your racial or class
maximum).

## Social Training 1-4

Interpersonal fluency, tradecraft in persuasion and deception. Each level increases your Charisma score by 1 (respecting
your racial or class maximum).

## Mental Saves Training 1-4

*Each level may be taken once per save (INT, WIS, or CHA).*

Hardening against mind-affecting threats: fear, manipulation, interrogation. Pick a save the first time you take Level
1; further levels apply to that same save. Take the track again from Level 1 for a different save.

- **Level 1:** Proficiency in the chosen save.
- **Level 2:** +1 to that save.
- **Level 3:** An additional +1 to that save (total +2 from this track).
- **Level 4:** Advantage on that save.

## Mental Skills Training 1-5

*Each level may be taken once per skill.*

General-purpose academic and social skill training. Pick a mental skill when you first take Level 1; further levels
apply to that same skill. Take the track again from Level 1 for a different skill.

Eligible skills: History, Investigation, Nature, Religion, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival,
Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion.

- **Level 1:** Proficiency in the chosen skill.
- **Level 2:** +1 to checks with that skill.
- **Level 3:** Once per long rest, reroll a critical failure (natural 1) on a check with that skill.
- **Level 4:** An additional +1 to checks (total +2).
- **Level 5:** The reroll is instead available once per short rest.

## Language Training 1-5

*Each level may be taken once per language.*

Fluency in a chosen language. Pick a language when you first take Level 1; further levels improve that language. Take
the track again from Level 1 for a different language. Each tier represents how complex a passage you can handle: simple
phrases pass at DC 10, working-level conversation at DC 15, nuance and jargon at DC 20. At your current tier, no check
is needed; above it, you roll the relevant check to get the message across.

| Task  | Ability Check |
|-------|---------------|
| Speak | Charisma      |
| Write | Dexterity     |
| Read  | Intelligence  |

- **Level 1 (Basic):** Handle simple exchanges (DC 10 content) without a check. More complex material requires the
  appropriate check.
- **Level 2 (Moderate):** Handle working-level conversation (DC 15 content) without a check.
- **Level 3 (Expert):** Handle nuanced, idiomatic, or technical material (DC 20 content) without a check.
- **Level 4 (Master):** You are fluent. No check is ever required for this language.
- **Level 5:** You can lip-read a speaker of that language if you can see their face clearly.
